We started seven8nine because we were tired

Tired of the agency model. Tired of bloated teams, endless meetings, and decks that go nowhere. We wanted something different.

We started seven8nine because we believed the traditional agency was broken. Too many layers, too many handoffs, too much bureaucracy between a great idea and its execution. We saw talented people stuck in systems that rewarded process over output and billing over brilliance.

So we built something leaner. A studio where every person on the team is senior, every project gets our full attention, and every deliverable reflects an obsessive level of craft. No juniors learning on your dime. No account managers playing telephone between you and the people actually doing the work.

Five years in, that founding frustration has become our greatest strength. We've shipped over 50 projects for brands across four continents — from scrappy startups to established enterprises. The common thread? Clients who refuse to settle for "good enough" and trust us to push further.

We're remote-first, globally distributed, and proudly small by design. Because staying small means staying sharp. It means every project matters. It means we can say no to work that doesn't excite us and pour everything into the work that does.
